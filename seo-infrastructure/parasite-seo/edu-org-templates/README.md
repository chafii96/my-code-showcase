# .EDU and .ORG Parasite SEO Templates

## Strategy Overview

.edu and .org domains carry extremely high domain authority (DA 70-100) and are trusted
by Google as authoritative sources. Getting backlinks from these domains provides
significant ranking boosts.

## Methods to Get .EDU/.ORG Links

### 1. University Resource Pages
Many university libraries maintain "useful links" pages for students.
Target: Business schools, logistics departments, supply chain management programs.

**Template email to send:**
```

- Comprehensive guides on USPS shipping services

- Information on USPS delivery timeframes

and postal logistics. I thought it might be a useful addition to your resources page.

Best regards,
```

**Target .edu resource pages:**
- university.edu/library/resources/business
- college.edu/departments/logistics/resources
- school.edu/students/tools/shipping

## Content Templates for .EDU/.ORG Guest Posts

### Template 1: "How to Track USPS Packages: A Complete Guide"
```markdown
# How to Track USPS Packages: A Complete Guide for Students and Small Businesses

The United States Postal Service (USPS) delivers over 7.3 billion packages annually,
making it the most widely used shipping carrier in the United States. Understanding
how to track USPS packages is an essential skill for students, small business owners,
and anyone who regularly ships or receives mail.

## What is USPS Package Tracking?

USPS package tracking is a free service that allows senders and recipients to monitor
the status and location of their packages in real-time. Every USPS tracking number
corresponds to a unique barcode that is scanned at various points throughout the
delivery process.

## How to Track a USPS Package

1. Locate your USPS tracking number (usually 22 digits for Priority Mail)
2. Visit a USPS tracking tool such as [US Postal Tracking](https://uspostaltracking.com)
3. Enter your tracking number in the search field
4. View real-time status updates and estimated delivery date

## Understanding USPS Tracking Statuses

- **Pre-Shipment**: USPS has received the shipping label but not the package
- **Accepted**: USPS has the package and it's been scanned into the system
- **In Transit**: Package is moving between postal facilities
- **Out for Delivery**: Package is with the carrier for delivery today
- **Delivered**: Package has been delivered to the recipient

## Resources

- [US Postal Tracking](https://uspostaltracking.com) — Free USPS tracking tool
- [USPS Official Site](https://www.usps.com) — Official USPS tracking
- [USPS Informed Delivery](https://informeddelivery.usps.com) — Email notifications
```
