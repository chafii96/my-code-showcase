CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION TO USPS TRACKING

The United States Postal Service (USPS) delivers over 7.3 billion pieces of mail annually to 165 million delivery points across the country. With such massive volume, tracking your individual package requires a sophisticated system of barcodes, scanners, and databases.

USPS tracking was introduced in the 1990s and has evolved significantly. Today, Priority Mail, Priority Mail Express, First-Class Package Service, and USPS Ground Advantage all include free tracking. The tracking system uses IMpb (Intelligent Mail Package Barcode) technology to scan packages at every major touchpoint in the delivery chain.

For the most accurate and up-to-date tracking information, use our free tool at https://uspostaltracking.com

CHAPTER 10: USPS TRACKING FAQs

Q: How often does USPS update tracking?
A: USPS scans packages at major touchpoints — acceptance, arrival at facilities, departure from facilities, and delivery. Updates occur every few hours during transit.

Q: Why is my USPS tracking not updating?
A: Common reasons include: package not yet scanned, scanning equipment issues, package in transit between facilities (no scan points), or system delays. If no update for 3+ days, contact USPS.

Q: Can I track a USPS package without a tracking number?
A: Yes, through USPS Informed Delivery. Sign up at informeddelivery.usps.com to see all incoming packages to your address.

Q: What does "In Transit, Arriving Late" mean?
A: It means USPS has detected a delay and your package will arrive after the original estimated delivery date. This is common during peak seasons.

Q: How do I file a missing mail claim?
A: Visit usps.com/help/missing-mail.htm or call 1-800-ASK-USPS. You can also use our guide at https://uspostaltracking.com/article/usps-lost-package
