## Why USPS Tracking Stops Updating

### 1. Package Hasn't Been Scanned Yet

The most common reason USPS tracking doesn't update is that your package simply hasn't been
scanned at the next facility yet. USPS packages are scanned at each facility they pass through,
but there can be gaps of 24-48 hours between scans, especially for:

- Ground Advantage packages (formerly USPS Retail Ground)
- Media Mail packages
- Packages traveling long distances
- Packages during peak seasons (holidays, back-to-school)

### 2. Package Is Between Facilities

When a package is physically moving between sorting facilities (on a truck or plane), it won't
receive any tracking scans. This can create gaps of 1-3 days in the tracking history.

### 3. Facility Delays

USPS sorting facilities can experience delays due to:
- High package volume
- Equipment issues
- Weather events
- Staffing shortages

### 4. The "Pre-Shipment" Status Issue

If your tracking shows "Pre-Shipment" or "Shipping Label Created, USPS Awaiting Item" for more
than 48 hours, it means the sender created a shipping label but may not have dropped off the
package yet. Contact your seller if this persists.

## How Long Can USPS Tracking Go Without Updating?

| Service | Normal Gap | Concerning Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Priority Mail Express | 4-8 hours | 24+ hours |
| Priority Mail | 12-24 hours | 48+ hours |
| First Class Package | 12-24 hours | 72+ hours |
| Ground Advantage | 24-48 hours | 5+ days |
| Media Mail | 24-72 hours | 7+ days |
| Parcel Select | 24-72 hours | 7+ days |

## What To Do When USPS Tracking Isn't Updating

### Step 1: Check the Full Tracking History

Visit [uspostaltracking.com](https://uspostaltracking.com) and enter your tracking number to see
the complete tracking history, including all scans and timestamps.

### Step 2: Wait the Appropriate Time

Based on the table above, wait the appropriate amount of time before taking action.

### Step 3: Contact USPS

If tracking hasn't updated beyond the "concerning gap" timeframe:
- Call USPS at 1-800-275-8777
- Visit your local post office
- File a Missing Mail search at usps.com

### Step 4: Contact the Sender

If you ordered from a retailer, contact them. They may have additional information or can
file a claim on your behalf.
